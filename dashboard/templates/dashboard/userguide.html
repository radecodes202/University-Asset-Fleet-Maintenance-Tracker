1. System Overview

FleetTrack is a centralized asset and fleet maintenance management system built for the university. It tracks the full lifecycle of every asset — from registration, to maintenance requests, to completed repairs — with a clear audit trail for accountability and transparency.

Dashboard
KPIs, totals, and recent activity at a glance.
Assets
Browse all registered vehicles & equipment.
Maintenance
Submit and track repair requests.

2. User Roles & Permissions

FleetTrack has three user roles. The system shows or hides navigation items based on your role.

Standard Staff

Default role for university employees.

  • View assets
  • Submit maintenance requests
  • Track own request status
  • Approve / reject requests
  • View costs or audit logs
Motorpool Manager

Administers the whole system.

  • Everything Staff can do
  • Approve / reject requests
  • Create & assign work orders
  • Record completed maintenance
  • Manage users & assets
  • View costs & audit logs
Auditor

Read-only oversight.

  • View assets
  • View all requests & history
  • View audit logs
  • Export reports (CSV)
  • Create or edit anything

3. The End-to-End Process Flow

Every maintenance job follows the same five-stage pipeline, from a staff member spotting a problem to the final entry in maintenance history. The diagram below shows how data moves through the system.

1
Request
Staff files a maintenance request for an asset.
→
2

→
3
Work Order
Manager assigns a technician via a work order.
→
4

Technician works on the asset and updates progress.
→
5

Why this matters: the pipeline guarantees that every repair is requested, approved, tracked, and recorded. Nothing is done informally and every step is auditable.

5. Standard Staff Guide

As a staff member your main job is to flag problems quickly and track them until they are fixed.

1
Sign in and open Maintenance

Use your university email and password on the login page. After signing in, click Maintenance in the sidebar.

2
Submit a new request

Click the Submit Request button (top-right). In the modal:

  • Pick the asset that has a problem from the dropdown.
  • Describe the problem in plain language — what's wrong, when it started, anything you've already tried.
  • Click Submit. The request is created with status PENDING.
3
Watch the status change

The same table shows all of your requests and their current status:

  • PENDING — waiting on a manager to review.
  • APPROVED — a manager approved it; a work order will follow.
  • REJECTED — a manager declined. Check the manager's notes for the reason.
  • COMPLETED — repair is finished and recorded in history.
4
Review the history

Open the History page to see what was actually done, the technician who did the work, and (for managers) the cost.

Tip: the more specific your problem description, the faster a manager can route it to the right technician.

6. Motorpool Manager Guide

As a manager, you are the gatekeeper of the maintenance pipeline. You approve requests, assign technicians, and close out the work.

1
Triage incoming requests

Open Maintenance. Filter by PENDING to see what needs your attention. For each one, click Approve or Reject.

When rejecting, add a short note explaining why — the requester will see it on their end.

2
Create a work order for approved requests

Open the Work Orders page. Create a new work order that:

  • Links to the approved maintenance request.
  • Assigns a technician from the staff list.
  • Includes a clear work description (e.g., "Replace front-left tire, rotate others, brake inspection").

Once created, the asset's status automatically changes to Under Maintenance.

3
Track work-in-progress

As the technician works, update the work order's status:

  • OPEN — created but not yet started.
  • IN PROGRESS — technician is on the job.
  • COMPLETED — work is done, ready to record in history.
  • CANCELLED — work abandoned (e.g., parts unavailable).
4
Close out the job in History

When the work order is completed, record the final details in History:

  • Final maintenance cost (₱).
  • Free-text remarks (parts replaced, follow-up notes, recommendations).
  • The asset's status automatically returns to Active.
5
Manage users and assets

Use the Users page to create new accounts, change roles, or deactivate former employees. Use the Assets page to register new equipment or mark items as Retired when they're out of service.

Reminder: only complete the close-out step after the technician has physically confirmed the repair. A history record is permanent.

7. Auditor Guide

Auditors have read-only access. Your job is to verify that the process is being followed, costs are reasonable, and nothing is being altered outside the system.

1
Review requests, work orders & history

Open Maintenance, Work Orders, and History to see the full record of every job that has run through the system. Use the search and status filters to narrow down to a specific time period, asset, or technician.

2
Drill into audit logs

Open Audit Logs. Every create, update, approve, reject, status change, login, and logout is recorded with the user, timestamp, IP address, and before/after values. Use this to:

  • Trace who approved a given request.
  • Verify that no manual edits happened outside the workflow.
  • Investigate suspicious patterns (e.g. multiple late-night approvals).
3
Export reports for off-system analysis

The History page has an Export CSV button. Use it to pull maintenance data into Excel or another reporting tool for periodic compliance reports.

Remember: your account is read-only. If you spot a process violation, raise it with the motorpool manager — do not attempt to modify any record from the UI.

10. Glossary

Asset — a university-owned vehicle or piece of equipment tracked in the system.

Maintenance Request — a formal request to repair an asset, filed by a staff member.

Work Order — an instruction to a named technician to perform the approved repair.

Technician — a staff member assigned to carry out a work order.

Maintenance History — the permanent log of completed jobs, including cost and remarks.

Audit Log — an immutable record of every action performed in the system.

Manager Notes — free-text comments a manager can attach to a request when approving or rejecting.

KPI — Key Performance Indicator (e.g., total assets, pending requests).
